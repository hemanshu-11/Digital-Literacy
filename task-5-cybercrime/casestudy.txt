# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Fraud?

"UPI fraud is a cyber crime in which fraudsters trick victims into sending money or revealing their UPI PIN using various deceptive methods."

## How Does UPI Fraud Happen? (Step by Step)

1. The fraudster contacts the victim via phone call or WhatsApp, posing as a bank representative or customer service representative.

2. The fraudster informs the victim that their bank account is compromised or that they have a cashback offer.

3. The fraudster sends a "collect request" via UPI applications such as GPay, PhonePe, or Paytm, which essentially means they're withdrawing money.

4. The fraudster then convinces the victim to enter their UPI PIN to verify their account or receive the cashback.

5. As soon as the PIN is entered, money is debited from the victim's account instantly.

## Who is Targeted?

- College students who are new to digital payments

- Senior citizens who are new to UPI

- Small business owners

- People searching for jobs or loans online

## Consequences

- Immediate loss of money

- Lack of an option to reverse the UPI transaction

- Mental stress and anxiety

- Possibility of identity theft if bank details are shared

- In some cases, the money lost is the entire savings

## Source

Based on the common pattern of UPI fraud cases reported on cybercrime.gov.in and RBI consumer awareness guidelines.
